Timing

How long should I cold-ferment pizza dough?

Most pizza dough cold-ferments best between 24 and 72 hours at fridge temperature (about 4 °C). Flavour deepens across that window; beyond about 4 days the dough starts to weaken.

Cold fermentation is the single biggest upgrade most home pizza-makers can make, and it costs nothing but time. Chilling the dough slows the yeast to a crawl while slow fermentation keeps building flavour — so you get taste without the dough racing ahead of you.

What's actually happening in the fridge

Here's the part most guides skip: your dough doesn't become fridge-cold the moment it goes in. A ball of dough takes several hours to cool through to the centre. During those first hours it's still fermenting at close to room speed, then it gradually slows as it chills. That "warm start" is why a good cold plan isn't simply "hours × cold rate" — and it's exactly what Doughborough's refrigerator model accounts for by integrating the cooling curve.

A practical window

  • 24 hours: noticeably better than same-day. Clean, mild flavour.
  • 48 hours: the sweet spot for most doughs — complex, easy to handle.
  • 72 hours: deepest flavour; watch that the dough still has structure.
  • Beyond 4 days: gluten begins breaking down; the dough can turn slack and sticky.

Let cold dough warm up before shaping — most cold pizza dough needs roughly 2–3 hours to warm, relax and finish proofing, though the right figure depends on style. Cold dough fights back and tears; relaxed dough stretches.
